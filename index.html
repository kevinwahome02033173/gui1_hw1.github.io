Prop Firm Trading

What is a Prop Firm?

A prop firm (short for proprietary trading firm) is a company that trades its own capital rather than client funds.

Difference between Institutional Prop Firms & Retail Prop Firms

Institutional Prop Firms

Institutional prop firms are trading firms/companies that trade their own capital directly through the financial markets trading stocks, futures, options, bonds, currencies, and other financial instruments.

Institutions use sophisticated trading strategies like high frequency trading, algorithmic trading, and quantitative trading to generate profits. They often have access to advanced technology and resources, allowing them to execute trades quickly and efficiently.

Professionals with formal backgrounds tend to work in these institutions and have access to their capital from day 1.

Retail Prop Firms

Retail prop firms similar to institutional prop firms also trade with their own capital however they do not hire professionals to trade their capital instead, what they do is create what are called "evaluation accounts" that can be accessed by anyone (even those with no formal education in trading) for a small fee (that vary from firm to firm).

Retail prop firms do not have the same level of capital, technology, and since they are dealing with retail traders with no formal education they tend to not have sophisticated trading strategies.
